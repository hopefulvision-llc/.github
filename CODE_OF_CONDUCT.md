# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior:
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Insults, derogatory comments, or exclusionary language
- Posting sexualized or violent content
- Repeatedly disrupting discussion or spamming
- Publishing others' private information without consent

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem unacceptable, up to and including removal from the project or organization.

## Reporting Guidelines

Report incidents to: cosimos.portinari@gmail.com

When reporting, please include:
- Which project/repo and where the incident occurred
- Time and date (approximate if unknown)
- The username(s) involved
- A short description and any supporting links or screenshots

Reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible and investigated promptly.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, mailing lists, chat channels, and in-person events organized by hopefulvision-llc.

## Enforcement

Consequences for unacceptable behavior may include:
- A private warning from maintainers
- Temporary or permanent removal of contributor privileges
- Public statement describing actions taken (when appropriate)
